Tourism in Australia

Tourism is one of the most important drivers of the Australian economy, attracting millions of domestic and international visitors every year. This dashboard provides an overview of tourism activity across Australia.

It highlights trends in travel, popular destinations, and the purposes of trips. Tourism generates significant income, creates jobs, and strengthens cultural ties with the rest of the world.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Number of Trips per City (1998 - 2016)

This visualization shows the distribution of trips by city across Australia, each circle represents a city, and the size of the circle corresponds to the total number of trips.

Larger circles indicate cities that attract a higher volume of tourists, such as Sydney and Melbourne, while smaller circles highlight destinations with more limited but still notable tourism activity.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

International Arrivals and Departures

On this visualization we can compare the international arrivals and departures from October 2015 to April 2025. The blue line represents arrivals, while the orange line represents departures.

For most of the period before 2020, arrivals and departures followed a similar seasonal pattern, with small fluctuations of travelers per month. Then in 2020 a big drop occured, reflecting the impact of the Covid 19 pandemic and border closures.

Travel remained near zero for over a year, before gradually recovering from 2021 onwards. By 2023, both arrivals and departures showed strong growth, reaching levels similar to the pre pandemic period

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Purpose of the Trip

Understanding why people travel to Australia is essential for analyzing the tourism industry.

Different purposes of travel such as holidays, visiting relatives, or business, highlight the diverse roles that tourism plays in the economy and society.

Looking at the breakdown of trip purposes helps identify which segments are the most significant and how they contribute to overall travel demand.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Tourist Seasonality by Quarters

Tourism activity is not evenly distributed throughout the year. Seasonal patterns play a significant role in shaping when and how people travel, with certain months and quarters consistently attracting more trips.

A clear seasonal rhythm can be observed, with stronger peaks in Q1 (the first quarter, which includes the Australian summer and holiday season) and Q4 (end-of-year holidays). Quarters Q2 and Q3 tend to record fewer trips in comparison.

Over time, the intensity of color also increases, especially toward the later years, reflecting the overall growth in tourism. This visualization highlights both the recurring seasonal cycles and the long-term upward trend in travel demand.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Top 10 Countries Visiting Australia

This visualisation highlights the top 10 countries of origin for visitors to Australia in the last three years.

China shows the strongest growth, rising from around 60,000 to nearly 100,000 visitors, confirming its position as a dominant tourism market. New Zealand remains consistently high, with over 110,000 visitors, making it one of the most important and stable sources of travelers

Other countries such as UK show steady increases, while countries like Hong Kong and India are growing more gradually but still trending upwards. In contrast, the USA shows a slight decline before recovering toward 2025.
